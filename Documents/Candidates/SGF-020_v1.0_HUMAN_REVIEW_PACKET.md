## Review findings

### Human and machine authority boundary

**Result: pass.**

Human, service, AI, and project-owner actors are distinguished. AI and service
actors may propose or execute deterministic operations but cannot satisfy a
human decision requirement.

### Authenticated identity and decision admission

**Result: pass.**

Material decisions require authenticated actor identity, target and prior
state, rationale, time, evidence and provenance, policy or method version,
uncertainty, independence where applicable, correction linkage, and an
immutable decision hash.

### Decision matrix coverage

**Result: pass.**

The matrix covers research scope, discovery, acquisition, screening,
extraction, evidence review, graph admission, theory review, alignment, bias,
validation, lifecycle, publication, indexing, monitoring, and AI-analysis
review.

### Separation of duties

**Result: pass.**

The candidate prevents AI self-review, service substitution for human review,
indexer manufacture of accepted state, administrative conversion into
scientific authority, and publication bypass. Consequential research requires
stronger independence and quorum.

### Freshness and historical preservation

**Result: pass.**

Content, evidence, method, policy, ethics, contradiction, and review-due
changes can make a decision stale without deleting history. Appeal,
correction, supersession, invalidation, and rollback are explicitly distinct.

### UI, API, and AI restrictions

**Result: pass.**

Required interfaces must show actor, target, prior state, effect, missing
evidence, stale context, policy gates, and append-only history. AI restrictions
are explicit and consistent with DOC-001 and SGF-000.

### Implementation honesty

**Result: pass with activation qualification.**

Existing controls, SGF-020A and SGF-020B increments, and required extensions
are kept separate. The document does not claim that multi-reviewer quorum,
appeal APIs, decision expiry, ethics references, or all identity-level
separation controls already exist.

### Downstream consistency

**Result: pass.**

No contradiction was found with the declared authority model used by SGF-030
or SGF-040. Ratification of SGF-020 would remove the authority dependency
blocker for SGF-030 but would not ratify SGF-030 itself.

## Activation qualification

standard. It does not prove that every required control is implemented.

Until the required extensions are verified, ResearchOS must not claim full
SGF-020 compliance for consequential medical, legal, safety-critical,
human-subject, or regulatory research. Missing authority or safety controls
must fail closed.

ResearchOS has not demonstrated an independent human governance reviewer.
Ratification by Jumadi would be a project-owner governance decision, not
independent peer review or scientific validation.
