## Material review conclusions

The v2.0 draft had a sound architectural direction but was not suitable for canonical admission because it created a parallel architecture-law identity, used conformance vocabulary inconsistent with the implemented engine, duplicated operational authority, left conceptual-to-implemented mappings incomplete, conflated logical and physical architecture, and had structural and layout defects.

The v2.1 candidate addresses those blockers by:

- treating Appendix A statements as candidate principles rather than active Architecture Laws;
- preserving implemented validator result enums and separating them from audit aggregation;
- referencing, rather than restating, operational and publication authority;
- mapping REA concepts to the implemented Graph, Fact, Law, Compliance, Review, ARC, and File Registry contracts;
- separating logical responsibility from revision-bound physical repository observations; and
- recording explicit scope, evidence, adoption, and change boundaries.
