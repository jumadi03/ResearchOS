## Proposed synthesis

> Stated willingness or favorable attitudes toward research data sharing do
> not by themselves establish frequent repository sharing practice.

This sentence is a human-authored cross-study synthesis. It is not presented
as a verbatim claim from either source.

2. `graph-f8e1bf752befa1741b74fa45`,
   `object-c3c7a0a143fca7d05996580a`, from the independent survey graph. The
   accepted result records mixed attitudes alongside a very high proportion

## Review criteria

- Citation fidelity: the synthesis must not imply that either study directly
  tested a causal relationship between attitude and repository behavior.
- Context preservation: willingness, attitude, and observed sharing practice
  must remain distinct constructs.
- Relevance: both results must materially constrain the same bounded
  proposition.
- Confidence: the conclusion is descriptive and cross-sectional; it is not a
  universal or causal theory.
- Independence: the reviewer must be different from the discoverer who
  proposed the synthesis.

## Recommendation

Accept only with the bounded reading that favorable stated disposition is
insufficient evidence, by itself, of frequent repository-sharing behavior.
Reject if the sentence is interpreted causally, universally, or as proving
that willingness has no relationship with later sharing.

## Required decision

The authorized human reviewer must choose `accept` or `reject` and provide a
rationale. Until then, the proposition remains `proposed`; ResearchOS will
not generate a theory bundle from it.
