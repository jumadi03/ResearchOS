# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in ResearchOS a respectful, harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity,
sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, experience, education,
socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or
sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive community include:

- demonstrating empathy, patience, and respect;
- giving and accepting constructive, evidence-based feedback;
- acknowledging mistakes and prioritizing the community's interests;
- respecting scientific disagreement without attacking individuals; and
- protecting credentials, personal data, and sensitive research material.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discriminatory language, threats,
deliberate disclosure of private information, credential exposure, sustained
disruption, or knowingly misrepresenting generated material as accepted
scientific evidence.

## Scope and enforcement

This policy applies in project spaces and when representing the project in
public. Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions and may
temporarily or permanently restrict participation for conduct they reasonably
consider inappropriate.

Report conduct concerns privately through the repository's security advisory
channel. Maintainers will protect the privacy and safety of reporters as far as
reasonably possible. Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented,
and free from conflicts of interest.
